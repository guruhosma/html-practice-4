Hyderabadi Veg Biryani

Aromatic, layered vegetarian biryani cooked "dum" style — fragrant basmati rice, spiced vegetables, saffron and fresh herbs.

Prep: 30 mins · Cook: 40 mins · Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked 30 min
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrot, potato, peas, beans)
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp biryani masala or garam masala
  • 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 2–3 green chilies, slit
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint, 1/4 cup chopped coriander
  • 8–10 saffron strands soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk
  • 3 tbsp ghee or oil, salt to taste
  • Whole spices: 2 bay leaves, 4 cloves, 3 green cardamom, 1" cinnamon, 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • Fried onions (optional) and roasted nuts for garnish

Marination

Mix vegetables with yogurt, biryani masala, turmeric, chili powder, half the chopped herbs, salt and a drizzle of oil. Let sit 15–20 minutes.

Par-boil Rice

  1. Boil water with salt and whole spices. Add soaked rice and cook until 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  2. Keep saffron milk ready and warm ghee.

Layering & Dum

  1. In a heavy-bottomed pot, heat oil/ghee and sauté sliced onions until golden. Remove half for garnish.
  2. Add marinated vegetables to the pot and cook briefly until they begin to soften.
  3. Spread half the par-boiled rice over the vegetables. Sprinkle half the saffron milk, some fried onions, mint and coriander.
  4. Repeat with remaining rice and toppings. Drizzle remaining saffron milk and ghee.
  5. Seal the pot with a tight lid (or dough) and cook on very low heat for 20–25 minutes (dum) — or place the pot on a griddle/tawa and cook for even heat.

Serving & Tips

  • Gently fluff before serving to preserve layers.
  • Serve with raita, salan (biryani curry) or a simple salad.
  • For richer flavor, use a mix of ghee and oil and add a few drops of rose water or kewra water.
